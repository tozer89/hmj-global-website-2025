Delivery model

A structured route from scope review to live contractor support

HMJ works best when recruitment is tied to delivery sequence rather than treated as a standalone admin task. The process below is how briefs stay grounded, mobilisation stays organised and contractors stay supported once they start.

01

Brief / scope review

We clarify package pressure, reporting lines, shift patterns and what the role needs to solve on site.

02

Shortlist & role fit

Candidates are screened against live delivery context, not just discipline keywords or headline titles.

03

Compliance & documentation

Right-to-work, insurance, site paperwork and supporting documents are organised before they become a bottleneck.

04

Onboarding & mobilisation

Travel, induction timing, accommodation expectations and site contacts are confirmed so starts feel controlled.

05

Payroll & live support

Once contractors are on site, HMJ stays involved through payroll, check-ins, extensions and continuity planning.

Market insights

What HMJ is seeing across data-centre, commissioning and regulated delivery hiring

These seeded briefings are written to reflect the conversations happening around mobilisation, retention and project readiness on live European programmes.

Commissioning outlook

Why commissioning talent is tightening across European programmes

Demand is no longer concentrated only in the lead roles. Projects are looking for people who can bridge install, turnover, systems interaction and handover readiness, often across multiple locations at once.

That puts pressure on availability, especially when schedules shift and teams need contractors who are documentation-ready as well as technically credible.

Mobilisation

What clients need in place before cross-border mobilisation

Offer acceptance is only one part of the journey. Projects move faster when right-to-work, insurance, onboarding windows and payroll expectations are discussed before travel is booked.

Payroll

Why payroll and onboarding matter so much on fast-track projects

Contractors will tolerate pressure better than uncertainty. Clear timesheet routes, payment cadence and pre-start communication often do as much for retention as the rate itself.

Support roles

Demand is rising beyond core engineering hires

Programmes increasingly need planners, document controllers, QA support, turnover admins and contractor-facing coordinators to keep package teams moving.

Retention

Project schedules have a direct effect on contractor retention

When start dates move repeatedly or site expectations are vague, confidence drops. The programmes that hold people best tend to communicate earlier and manage changes more cleanly.

Pharma / clean utilities

Documentation-ready hires are increasingly important on regulated packages

Technical fit still matters, but projects are putting more value on people who understand structured procedures, change control and the realities of validated environments.

Compliance, Standards & Professional Conduct

We operate to the standards that mission-critical programmes demand.

The compliance model behind HMJ is designed to support live programmes across the UK and Europe with practical, documented, and transparent operating discipline.

REC (Recruitment & Employment Confederation)

HMJ Global operates in line with the REC Code of Professional Practice — the UK's standard for ethical and legally compliant recruitment. The Code covers conduct, legal obligations, candidate treatment, and client transparency. We are working towards full REC membership.

IR35 & Off-Payroll Compliance

For every UK contractor engagement, we issue a documented Status Determination Statement (SDS) in line with Chapter 10, ITEPA 2003. Inside-IR35 roles are handled via compliant umbrella PAYE arrangements. We do not place contractors outside IR35 without a defensible assessment. Clients receive full documentation on request.

GDPR & Data Protection

HMJ-Global Ltd processes candidate and client data under the UK GDPR and EU GDPR (Regulation 2016/679). We maintain a Data Protection Policy, retain data only for specified purposes, and provide data subject rights on request. Our Privacy Policy is published and kept current.

Cross-border payroll compliance — We operate employer-of-record and payroll partnerships across 12 EU markets, covering social contributions, local employment law, and in-country onboarding. Our EU compliance infrastructure has been built and tested across live programmes in Ireland, the Netherlands, Germany, Finland, and beyond.
